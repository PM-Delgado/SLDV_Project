Discrimination in Portugal

CHEGA focus their discriminatory speeches towards the Romani ethnicity, however, it isn't restricted to that. Both immigrants (specially those coming from Brazil) and black skin colored people are also discriminated by the party.

It is evident that since Chega's appearance, the number of registered discrimination complaints rose, reaching a more or less stable number above 300 complaints per year, where the majority of it has at its basis: the Romani ethnicity, Brazilian nationality and Black skin color.

Even though the Romani ethnicity complaints didn't increase since 2019 (when it was at its peak), it is important to note that most of the complaints about that group are made by third parties and not the victims themselves (in contrast with the Brazilian group). Therefore, with the existing data, it's safe to say that the real number of discrimination may be way higher that the number of complaints. This can mean that that minority group simply doesn't have access to the information and tools needed to report such discrimation crimes by themselves.

All the data used in this project was taken from Portuguese Parlament's official website and from the Commission for Equality and Against Racial Discrimination.
